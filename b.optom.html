Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom)

A comprehensive 4-year undergraduate program designed to train students in eye care, vision science, and ocular disease management.

Apply Now

Course Overview

Program Description

The Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom) is a professional degree program that prepares students for careers in eye care. The program combines theoretical knowledge with practical training in vision care, ocular disease diagnosis, contact lens fitting, and low vision rehabilitation.

Program Highlights

  • 4-year program with 1-year compulsory internship
  • State-of-the-art optometry labs and equipment
  • Clinical training from 2nd year onwards
  • Industry collaborations for practical exposure
  • Placement assistance after graduation

Learning Outcomes

Graduates will be able to perform comprehensive eye examinations, diagnose visual problems, prescribe corrective lenses, detect eye diseases, provide vision therapy, and fit contact lenses. They will also develop skills in patient communication and practice management.

Duration & Degree

The B.Optom program spans 4 academic years (8 semesters) followed by a 1-year compulsory rotating internship. Upon successful completion, students are awarded the Bachelor of Optometry degree, making them eligible to register as optometrists with the appropriate regulatory body.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Passed 10+2 or equivalent examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics
  • Minimum aggregate marks of 50% in PCB/PCM (45% for reserved categories)
  • Candidates must have completed 17 years of age at the time of admission
  • Qualifying entrance examination may be required (varies by institution)
  • Medical fitness certificate is mandatory

Career Prospects

Clinical Optometrist

Work in hospitals, eye clinics, or optical outlets performing eye examinations, prescribing corrective lenses, and detecting eye diseases.

Contact Lens Specialist

Specialize in fitting and managing contact lenses for patients with various vision needs and ocular conditions.

Low Vision Specialist

Work with patients who have significant vision impairment, helping them maximize their remaining vision with specialized devices.

Optical Industry Professional

Work with optical manufacturers in product development, sales, marketing, or training roles.

Academic & Researcher

Pursue teaching positions in optometry colleges or engage in vision science research.

Entrepreneur

Start your own optical retail outlet, eye clinic, or specialized vision care center.
