Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA)

4-Year Undergraduate Program

Program Overview

The Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA) is a 4-year undergraduate program designed to provide students with a strong foundation in computer applications and software development. Our curriculum blends theoretical knowledge with practical skills, preparing students for careers in the rapidly evolving IT industry.

Program Highlights: Industry-aligned curriculum, hands-on training, experienced faculty, internship opportunities, and placement assistance.

Program Objectives

  • Develop strong programming and problem-solving skills
  • Provide comprehensive knowledge of computer systems and applications
  • Foster understanding of software development lifecycle
  • Enhance analytical and logical thinking abilities
  • Prepare students for higher studies and research in computer science
  • Develop professional skills for the IT industry

Course Structure

Year 1: Foundation

  • Introduction to Programming
  • Computer Fundamentals
  • Mathematics for Computing
  • Digital Electronics
  • Communication Skills
  • Web Design Basics

Year 2: Core Concepts

  • Data Structures
  • Object-Oriented Programming
  • Database Management Systems
  • Operating Systems
  • Computer Networks
  • Discrete Mathematics

Year 3: Advanced Topics

  • Algorithms
  • Software Engineering
  • Web Development
  • Mobile Application Development
  • Cloud Computing
  • Elective I

Year 4: Specialization & Project

  • Artificial Intelligence
  • Data Science
  • Cybersecurity
  • Elective II
  • Major Project
  • Internship

Elective Courses

Students can choose from the following elective courses in their third and fourth years:

Elective Description
Game Development Principles of game design and development using modern engines
Blockchain Technology Fundamentals of blockchain and cryptocurrency systems
IoT Systems Design and implementation of Internet of Things solutions
UI/UX Design User interface and experience design principles
Big Data Analytics Processing and analyzing large datasets

Admission Requirements

  • Successful completion of 10+2 or equivalent examination
  • Minimum 50% aggregate marks in qualifying examination
  • Mathematics as a subject in 10+2 is preferred
  • Qualifying entrance examination (if applicable)
